Boards

There are 5 major snowboard shapes which all vary in size, weight, flex, and shape. "Freeride" boards are good for all snow conditions. "Freestyle" boards are playfull and for the park. "Powder" boards allow you to float over soft snow. "Split boards" are best for backcountry. And finally "All Mountain" boards are a perfect mix of everything.

History

Modern snowboarding began in 1965 when Sherman Poppen, an engineer in Muskegon, Michigan, invented a toy for his daughter by fastening two skis together and attaching a rope to one end so she would have some control as she stood on the board and glided downhill.

Events

Snowboarding has been largely clouded by skiing yet it is still present in many major competitions such as X Games and the Olympics. Events at competitions typically include, racing, park, big air, half-pipe, slalom, slopestyle, and freestyle.
